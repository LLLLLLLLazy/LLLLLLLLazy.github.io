## About Me

I am an undergraduate student at Northwestern Polytechnical University. My interests center on computer architecture, operating systems, and efficient decoding algorithms. I enjoy building and evaluating complete systems, from RTL design and FPGA validation to algorithm implementation and experimental analysis.

## Research Interests

- **Computer Systems:** processor microarchitecture, hardware-software co-design, and operating systems
- **Quantum Error Correction:** low-latency and parallel decoding for quantum LDPC codes
- **Reliable AI Systems:** retrieval-augmented generation and domain-specific knowledge systems

## Research Experience

<div class="entry" markdown="1">

### Low-Latency Parallel-Window Decoding for Quantum qLDPC Codes

<span class="entry-meta">Algorithm Design and Experimental Evaluation</span>

- Studying real-time decoding under circuit-level noise using a parallel-window framework.
- Designed a candidate-selection strategy combining matrix partitioning, joint-B dynamic programming, and gated interfaces to reduce boundary-error propagation while preserving small-window parallelism.
- Built experiments comparing logical error rate and runtime against conventional sliding-window and standard parallel-window baselines.

</div>

## Selected Projects

<div class="entry" markdown="1">

### LoongArch32 Dual-Issue Pipelined Processor

<span class="entry-meta">Team Lead · Loongson Cup National Student Computer System Capability Challenge</span>

- Led a team in designing an in-order, dual-issue, eight-stage processor from the ground up for the reduced 32-bit LoongArch instruction set.
- Implemented instruction decoding and dual-issue scheduling in Verilog, and led microarchitecture design and FPGA validation with Vivado.
- Reached **90 MHz** on FPGA, passed all official functional and performance tests with a score of **2.142**, and booted Linux through U-Boot network loading.

</div>
